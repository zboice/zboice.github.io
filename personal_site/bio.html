I'm a third year undergraduate at SUNY Geneseo, studying Physics and Math. In my spare time, I am the president of Geneseo's Physics and Astronomy Club, I sing in an acapella group, and I play the cello. I also enjoy learning about random physics topics and then creating simulations or visualizations.

For the past two years, I've been doing research with Dr. Thomas Osburn on gravitational wave source modeling. Importantly, I implemented a hyperboloidal slicing and compactification scheme which improved boundary conditions. Our current work is on broadening this scheme to easily accomodate eccentric geodesics, and to eventually allow for inspirals.
